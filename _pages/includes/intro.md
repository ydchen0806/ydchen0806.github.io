I am **Yinda Chen (陈胤达)**, a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Science and Technology of China, jointly trained with Shanghai AI Lab.

My research focuses on **predictive multimodal intelligence**: learning transferable representations from large-scale and weakly annotated data, modeling structured spatial-temporal dynamics, and using these predictions for generation, reasoning, scientific discovery, and decision-making.

I currently work across two connected domains. In **embodied intelligence**, I study multimodal foundation models, world models, and vision-language-action systems that connect perception, prediction, and action. In **computational neuroscience**, I develop learning methods for connectomics, large-scale electron microscopy, and neural circuit analysis.

I am exploring **2027 industry research positions** in multimodal foundation models, world models, embodied AI, and AI for Science, as well as **postdoctoral opportunities** in computational neuroscience, connectomics, and NeuroAI.
